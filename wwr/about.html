History

Fremer Water Rafting traces its inspiration from the long history of white-water rafting, an adventure activity that began as a practical way for people to travel and transport goods across rivers. Early forms of rafting were used centuries ago with wooden rafts and canoes, but modern rafting began developing in the 1840s when rubber rafts were introduced in the United States. By the 1940s and 1950s, rafting became a popular recreational sport on rivers like the Colorado and Salmon Rivers. Over time, improved safety equipment and growing adventure tourism helped rafting spread worldwide as an exciting outdoor activity enjoyed for teamwork, exploration, and thrill-seeking
